## Dual Track: IC vs Management

| Aspecto | Individual Contributor (IC) | Management |
|---------|---------------------------|------------|
| **Impact** | Through technical excellence | Through people and teams |
| **Skills** | Deep technical, system design | Leadership, strategy, people |
| **Day-to-day** | Code, design, review | 1:1s, planning, hiring |
| **Growth** | Depth/breadth of expertise | Scope of responsibility |
| **Compensation** | Can reach Staff+ levels | Similar comp at director+ |

### Why Dual Track Matters

- Not all great engineers want to manage
- Forcing IC into management loses technical talent
- Clear IC path retains top individual contributors
- Both tracks should have equal prestige and compensation

## Typical Leveling Framework

### IC Track

| Level | Title | Scope | Impact |
|-------|-------|-------|--------|
| L3 | Junior Engineer | Individual tasks with guidance | Learn and contribute |
| L4 | Software Engineer | Independent features | Own deliverables |
| L5 | Senior Engineer | Multiple features / subsystems | Team-level impact |
| L6 | Staff Engineer | Cross-team technical direction | Org-level impact |
| L7 | Principal Engineer | Company-wide technical strategy | Company-level impact |
| L8 | Distinguished Engineer | Industry-level influence | Industry-level impact |

### Management Track

| Level | Title | Scope | Reports |
|-------|-------|-------|---------|
| M1 | Engineering Manager | 1 team (5-8 engineers) | Direct reports |
| M2 | Senior EM / Director | Multiple teams | EMs + ICs |
| M3 | VP Engineering | Department | Directors + EMs |
| M4 | CTO / SVP Engineering | Company-wide | VPs + Directors |

## Promotion Criteria Matrix

### IC Promotion: Senior (L4 → L5)

| Dimension | Expectation | Evidence |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Technical** | Design features end-to-end | ADRs, system design docs |
| **Execution** | Delivers complex features independently | PRs, shipped features |
| **Impact** | Improves team practices | Process improvements, tool adoption |
| **Communication** | Clear written and verbal | Design docs, tech talks |
| **Mentoring** | Helps junior engineers grow | Code reviews, mentoring sessions |

### IC Promotion: Staff (L5 → L6)

| Dimension | Expectation | Evidence |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Technical** | Sets technical direction for team/org | Tech strategy docs, architecture |
| **Execution** | Delivers ambiguous, large-scale projects | Multi-quarter projects |
| **Impact** | Influences multiple teams | Cross-team initiatives |
| **Communication** | Influences through writing | Company-wide ADRs, blog posts |
| **Leadership** | Technical leadership without authority | Leading working groups |

## Calibration Process

### Calibration Meeting Structure

| Phase | Duration | Activity |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **Prep** | 1 week | Managers prepare packets with evidence |
| **Self-nomination** | 1 week | Engineers submit promotion packets |
| **Manager discussion** | 2 hours | Discuss each candidate, calibrate across teams |
| **VP review** | 1 hour | Cross-org calibration, final decisions |
| **Communication** | 1 week | Individual feedback sessions |

### Calibration Guidelines

- **Evidence-based**: Promotions based on demonstrated impact, not tenure
- **Cross-team calibration**: Ensure consistency across managers
- **Bias check**: Review for demographic patterns in promotions
- **Async-friendly**: Written packets enable thoughtful discussion
- **Timely feedback**: Communicate decisions within 2 weeks

## Common Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Patrón | Consecuencia |
|-------------|-------------|
| "Up or out" culture | Unnecessary pressure, attrition |
| Manager track as only path | Technical talent leaves |
| Level inflation | Confusion, compensation issues |
| No clear criteria | Perception of unfairness |
| Tenure-based promotions | Mediocrity rewarded |
| Solo calibration (no cross-check) | Manager bias, inconsistency |
