|-----------|---------|--------|
| **Financial** | +19% más revenue (McKinsey) | Diversity Wins 2020 |
| **Innovation** | +19% más innovation revenue | BCG, 2018 |
| **Decision making** | +87% mejor decision making | Cloverpop |
| **Retention** | +22% menor turnover | Deloitte |
| **Employer brand** | Top talent attraction | LinkedIn research |

## Modelos de Diversidad

| Tipo | Definición | Ejemplos |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Visible** | Diferencias percibidas | Género, etnia, discapacidad |
| **Invisible** | Diferencias no percibidas | Orientación, neurodiversidad, religión |
| **Experiencial** | Background y experiencias | Educación, geografía, industry |
| **Cognitive** | Estilos de pensamiento | Analytical vs creative, risk tolerance |

### Inclusion vs Diversity

- **Diversity** = Who is in the room
- **Inclusion** = Whose voices are heard
- **Belonging** = Who feels they can be themselves

> "Diversity is being invited to the party. Inclusion is being asked to dance. Belonging is being able to choose the music." — Vernā Myers

## Inclusive Hiring

### Job Description Audit

| Check | Action |
|-------|--------|
| Gendered language | Run through Textio or Gender Decoder |
| Requirements inflation | Separate "must-have" from "nice-to-have" |
| Credential bias | Consider skills-based hiring over degree requirements |
| Culture fit vs culture add | Reframe to "culture contribution" |

### Structured Interview Process

| Phase | Practice |
|-------|----------|
| **Screening** | Blind resume review (remove names, photos) |
| **Phone screen** | Standardized questions for all candidates |
| **Technical** | Consistent coding challenge across candidates |
| **Behavioral** | Structured interview with rubric scoring |
| **Culture** | Assess "culture add" not "culture fit" |
| **Debias** | Panel interview, score independently before discussing |

### Interview Scorecard Template

| Criterion | 1 (Weak) | 3 (Meets) | 5 (Strong) | Score |
|-----------|----------|-----------|------------|-------|

## Belonging at Work

### The 4 Dimensions of Belonging

| Dimension | Description | Signal |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Welcomed** | People are glad I'm here | Greetings, inclusion in activities |
| **Known** | People know my authentic self | Personal conversations, interest in me |
| **Accepted** | People accept my differences | No pressure to conform |
| **Supported** | People have my back | Advocacy, sponsorship, help |

### Belonging Practices

| Practice | Implementation |
|----------|---------------|
| **Pronouns** | Share in Slack profiles, email signatures |
| **Meeting facilitation** | Round-robin, async input, no interruption |
| **Social events** | Alcohol-free options, varied activities |
| **Documentation** | Multiple formats (video, text, visual) |
| **Feedback** | Structured, bias-aware feedback processes |

## Inclusive Meetings

| Practice | Why it matters |
|----------|---------------|
| **Pre-read** | Async people can prepare and contribute |
| **Round-robin** | Ensures everyone speaks, not just loudest |
| **"No interruptions" rule** | Protects quieter voices |
| **Written follow-up** | Captures ideas from non-verbal contributors |
| **Rotate facilitator** | Distributed ownership |
| **Time zone awareness** | Fair meeting times across regions |

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Patrón | Consecuencia |
|-------------|-------------|
| Performative diversity (optics over substance) | Cynicism, no real change |
| "Color-blind" approach | Ignores real systemic issues |
| Burden on minorities to educate | Tokenism, exhaustion |
| One-time training = done | Checkbox mentality |
| Ignoring intersectionality | Overlooks compounded challenges |
